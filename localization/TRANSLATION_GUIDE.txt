These localization lines are for locations in heists- they are usually displayed as:
PLAYERNAME STATE LOCATION
* where PLAYERNAME is the player's display username, 
* STATE reflects the player's in-game state (eg. "tased", "downed", or if no special states are applied, is left empty: ""; See above for a list of state localizations.)
* and LOCATION is the name of the place that the player is at while in the heist.

Example:
	Offyerrocker DOWNED In Canteen.
	Dallas TASED On Floor 2.
	Wolf INCAP. 2F Bathroom
	Chains Outside Store

For the heists originating from PAYDAY: The Heist, the location names are reused from that game;
For the rest of the heists from PAYDAY 2, I just made up the location names based on the location itself.
If you don't know what these places are in-game, I recommend exploring them and finding names that fit in the language you are translating.

Some names are inconsistent between heists: 
for example, sometimes an area on the first floor is abbreviated to "1F", and sometimes it is written as "Floor 1".
Some inconsistencies already existed in the location names in PAYDAY: The Heist,
so I am not too concerned about making the names completely consistent everywhere- they only need to be consistent within the same heist so as not to confuse players.

Here are some naming conventions and notes for the way I have made the location names (it is up to you how closely to follow them):
- I often abbreviate cardinal directions "North", "South", "East", and "West" to "N", "S", "E", or "W".
- I often abbreviate floor numbers, for example "Floor 1" --> "1F". This is particularly important for more description location names.
- In the USA, the ground floor is known as the first (1st) floor; I'm aware that in some other countries, the ground floor is the ground floor, and the floor above that is numbered starting at 1.
	Some other countries may have different systems, and sometimes this may be confusing since Bain/Locke (the heist narrators) may refer to floors in the USA style, since the game does largely take place in the USA. 
	Having never played a version of PAYDAY 2 in a different language, I don't know which way would be more consistent, so it is up to you as the translator.
- Where possible, I will use the names that Bain/Locke or the preplanning map refer to them as, for consistency. Since OVERKILL does not use the preplanning system very much anymore, this isn't available on every map, though.
